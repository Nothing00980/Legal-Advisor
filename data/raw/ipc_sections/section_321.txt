Concealment of birth by secret disposal of dead body.
Whoever, by secretly burying or otherwise disposing of the dead body of a child
whether such child die before or after or during its birth, intentionally conceals or
endeavours to conceal the birth of such child, shall be punished with imprisonment of
either description for a term which may extend to two years, or with fine, or with both.
COMMENT.—
This section is intended to prevent infanticide. It is directed against concealment of
birth of a child by secretly disposing of its body.
[s 318.1] Ingredients.—
The section requires—
(1) Secret burying or otherwise disposing of the dead body of a child.
(2) It is immaterial whether such child dies before or after or during its birth.
(3) Intention to conceal the birth of such child by such secret burying or disposal.
