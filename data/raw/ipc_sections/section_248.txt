Unlawfully taking coining instrument from mint.
Whoever, without lawful authority, takes out of any mint, lawfully established in 34.
[India], any coining tool or instrument, shall be punished with imprisonment of either
description for a term which may extend to seven years, and shall also be liable to
fine.
34. The words "British India" have successively been subs. by the A.O. 1948, the A.O. 1950 and
Act 3 of 1951, section 3 and Sch. (w.e.f. 1 April 1951), to read as above.
