Making or possession of any instrument for counterfeiting a property
mark.
Whoever makes or has in his possession any die, plate or other instrument for the
purpose of counterfeiting a property mark, or has in his possession a property mark
for the purpose of denoting that any goods belong to a person to whom they do not
belong, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which
may extend to three years or with fine, or with both.
COMMENT.—
The making or possession of instruments for counterfeiting a property mark is hereby
punished.
108. Amendment.—The word "trade" has been omitted by the Trade and Merchandise Marks
Act, 1958 (Act XLIII of 1958), section 135 and Sch. The Act came into force on 25 November
1959.
