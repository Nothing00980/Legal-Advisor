rupees.Mischief by killing or maiming animal of the value of ten rupees.
Whoever commits mischief by killing, poisoning, maiming or rendering useless any
animal or animals of the value of ten rupees or upwards, shall be punished with
imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to two years, or with
fine, or with both.
COMMENT—
This section is intended to prevent cruelty to animals and consequent loss to the
owner.
[s 428.1] 'Maiming'.—
refers to those injuries which cause the privation of the use of a limb or a member of
the body.635. 'Maiming' implies a permanent injury,636. wounding is not necessarily
maiming.
