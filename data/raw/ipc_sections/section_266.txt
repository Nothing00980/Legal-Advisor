Erasure of mark denoting that stamp has been used.
Whoever, fraudulently or with intent to cause loss to Government, erases or removes
from a stamp issued by the Government for the purpose of revenue, any mark, put or
impressed upon such stamp for the purpose of denoting that the same has been used,
or knowingly has in his possession or sells or disposes of any such stamp from which
such mark has been erased or removed, or sells or disposes of any such stamp which
he knows to have been used, shall be punished with imprisonment of either
description for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both.
