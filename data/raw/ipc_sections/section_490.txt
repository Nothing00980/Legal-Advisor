Property mark.
A mark used for denoting that movable property belongs to a particular person is
called a property mark.
COMMENT.—
The distinction between 'trade mark' and 'property mark' is not recognised in English
law.
108. Amendment.—The word "trade" has been omitted by the Trade and Merchandise Marks
Act, 1958 (Act XLIII of 1958), section 135 and Sch. The Act came into force on 25 November
1959.
